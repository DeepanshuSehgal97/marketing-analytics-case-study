# Marketing Analytics Case Study

A 12-month analysis of channel, product, and geography performance for an online travel marketplace. End-to-end SQL queries on a Vertica-style warehouse and a Python notebook on aggregated daily performance data, with a written report and recommendations.

> **Context:** This is a portfolio case study based on an analytical assignment completed during an interview process. The company name and any identifying details have been anonymised; the structure of the data and the brief are preserved.

## The brief

A digital travel platform invests heavily in paid acquisition (SEM, META) alongside organic channels (DIRECT, SEO). The task was to:

1. Write SQL queries to compute channel-level monthly performance, identify the top markets for new SEM customers, and calculate LTV for a country cohort.
2. Load a daily-performance CSV in Python, compute RPB and Take Rate per channel and country with within-country rankings, and visualise monthly spend.
3. Extract relevant business insights from the past 12 complete months.

## Headline findings

- **HOTEL is the most underused product** in the portfolio: best margin (59.1%) and ROI (2.44x), but only 9.3% of profit.
- **FLIGHT + HOTEL is the profit engine:** 70.5% of profit from 20.6% of bookings, at EUR 190 RPB.
- **The OTHER geography segment is dragging the average down:** 32.9% of bookings, worst margin (31.6%), and 68% of revenue consumed by spend.
- **June 2025 SEM blew up:** spend +50.8% delivered only +18.7% revenue growth, cutting overall margin from 50.5% to 37.1%. Four combinations alone accounted for EUR 544K of losses.
- **Organic channels carry the business:** DIRECT and SEO together deliver 63.8% of profit on under EUR 1K of total spend.

## How to reproduce

The Python notebook runs end-to-end against `data/daily_performance.csv`:

```bash
pip install pandas matplotlib jupyter
jupyter notebook python/channel_country_analysis.ipynb
```

The SQL queries are written against the schema described in the brief (`bookings` and `customers` tables) and can be run against any Vertica-compatible warehouse.
